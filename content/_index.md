    <h1>Rahul Devajji</h1>
    <p class="home-tagline">PhD researcher · Urban climate · Geospatial AI · Indiana University Bloomington</p>
    <p>I'm a PhD researcher in the <a href="https://healthycities.sice.indiana.edu">Healthy Cities Lab</a>, advised by Dr. Dana Habeeb. I study where heat concentrates in cities, who is most vulnerable, and how practical tools can help communities reduce risk.</p>
    <p>Before the PhD, I worked as a software engineer. That still shapes my research: I like building usable systems, from sensor pipelines and dashboards to mapping tools for local decision-makers.</p>
    <p>My research focuses on extreme heat and heat-health risk, with methods spanning Earth observation, geospatial ML, and real-time sensor networks. I care a lot about translation: taking technical methods and turning them into practical systems that help cities prepare for heat.</p>
    <p>Outside the lab, I read a lot of science fiction, play tennis, and listen to too many podcasts. The <a href="/readings/">books</a> page is where I keep track of what I've been reading.</p>
