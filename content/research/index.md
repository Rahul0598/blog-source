I am a PhD researcher in Informatics at Indiana University's [Healthy Cities Lab](https://healthycities.sice.indiana.edu), advised by Dr. Dana Habeeb. I use geospatial AI and public-health data to map urban heat risk and build tools cities can use.

<div class="research-columns">
  <section>
    <h2>Current Work</h2>
    <ul class="research-list">
      <li><strong>Temperature prediction:</strong> hourly citywide air-temperature models from sparse sensors and AEF embeddings.</li>
      <li><strong>Local Climate Zones:</strong> LCZ classification in Google Earth Engine, validated against hyperlocal sensors. <a href="https://iu.maps.arcgis.com/home/item.html?id=22673fb225b844a38c2cb59e07944fe9">ArcGIS</a>.</li>
      <li><strong>Heat-health analysis:</strong> models of heat-related ED visits using hospital, weather, and census data.</li>
      <li><strong>Heat planning tools:</strong> municipal HVI and sensor dashboards. <a href="https://healthycities.luddy.indiana.edu/beattheheat/hvmt/richmond.html">HVI</a> · <a href="https://healthycities.luddy.indiana.edu/sensor/city.html">sensors</a>.</li>
    </ul>
  </section>
