The concept of first contact, but with plants as the dominant life form instead of animals, is quite interesting. It instantly reminded me of *Tchaikovsky's* "Children of Time," which I absolutely loved. Reading about plants communicating with each other and sharing resources is interesting. But I would have loved for it to have dived deeper into the science of how these alien plants work. I think there is a line between being science-heavy, like the "Three Body Problem" trilogy (making it read like a memorandum), and being a fantasy book where all the "science" is just make-believe, and I wish this book had explored more of the plant physiology. Also, towards the end, the main plant protagonist became too human, with feelings of guilt, which made it less immersive than I had hoped for.
