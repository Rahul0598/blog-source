Okay, I read this book in almost 6 months ago (at the time of writing this review on 03/2026). Looking back on it, I feel I learnt nothing. The author argues put together some shower thoughts, cited some old research, and called it done. I guess it is pretty short because he doesn't have enough things to say about this topic, it is even more evident based on how he decides to end the book *"I’m hungry, yes, but it also seems that I’ve made my point. In fact, I can’t think of anything else to say on the subject. And where is the freedom in that?"*. I think it was predetermined that there is no free-will, so this book was written to find ways to say "hence-proved" in the end.
